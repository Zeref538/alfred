ALFRED — TAB BRIDGE

Paste the address Alfred printed when he started (the one with ?t=). It carries the port and this session's key.

What this sends: the title and address of your open tabs, to 127.0.0.1 only — never off this machine. Alfred keeps only the title and the hostname; paths and query strings are discarded on arrival and never stored.

What it cannot do: read the contents of any page. It holds no scripting permission, so page text, forms and passwords are out of reach.

To blind him to a site, add it to ~/.alfred/tab_privacy.yaml. Banking and sign-in pages are excluded already. Pause stops all reporting at once.
